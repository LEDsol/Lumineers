Should I throw away all my ordinary light-bulbs and replace them with LEDs?

Answered Sep 29
MY calculations say that an 100 W incandescent will burn thru as much as $20 of electricity in its expected life operation.
So clearly there’s a case for replacing every incandescent with a $5. 10, or even $15 LED bulb that will in the lifespan of the incandescent save enough to pay for itself burning maybe $4 of electricity. And that won’t need to be replaced for probably 8–10 incandescent lifespans saving replacement costs and also paying for itself in 16 dollars savings for each incandescent lifspan.
But if you have it in the attic and turn it on for five minutes every month, you probably won’t burn out that incandescent or use all that much electricity. Seldom used lights probably don’t need to be replaced.
If you replace incandescents with LED, do someone a favor and throw them out, don’t donate them to a charity and cost that charity $20 to run it.
